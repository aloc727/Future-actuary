Below is a deterministic synthetic book of 10,000 AI-assisted prior-authorization decisions for a Medicare Advantage post-acute population — the setting of the real nH Predict litigation. Every number is computed in your browser from js/actuarial.js; there is no backend. At the default settings these figures match Section 6 of the framework paper. Move the controls to stress the book and watch the governance metrics respond.

What this means: Each error is not equal. The blue mass is routine; the red tail beyond VaRα is where the lawsuits live. TVaRα is the average cost of a decision once you are in that tail — the number a single accuracy figure can never show.

2 · Actual-to-Expected drift study
replaces one-time validation

What this means: Green cohorts behave as the model was validated (A/E ≈ 1.0). Red cohorts have drifted: reality is producing far more errors than expected. A single portfolio accuracy number averages these together and stays silent — the A/E study names the victims.

What this means: Z = n/(n+k) is the weight a cohort's own experience earns. A small cohort's alarming raw rate gets pulled toward the portfolio base rate — you don't bet the firm on thin evidence, and you don't ignore it either.

4 · IBNR reserve & economic capital
replaces "no reserve, no capital"

What this means: Ground truth lags, so today's adjudicated errors are only part of the story. A loss-development factor projects the ultimate errors; the gap is IBNR — failures already baked in but not yet surfaced — which you must reserve for. Economic capital (TVaR − expected loss) is the buffer for the tail beyond the reserve. The portfolio capital figure is an undiversified upper bound (see paper §8).

🔍
Reproducibility & invariants. The portfolio is generated from a fixed seed, so these numbers are byte-for-byte reproducible and match the paper. By construction the tool always satisfies TVaR ≥ VaR, reserve ≥ 0, Z increasing in n, and A/E ≈ 1.0 when drift = 0. Verify them yourself: drag Production drift to 0% and watch every cohort collapse onto the A/E = 1.0 line.
